# HVAC Contractor — Landing Page Template Structure

> **Primary goal:** Convert visitors into calls, form submissions, or booked consultations.
> **Visitor psychology:** HVAC visitors fall into three modes — **emergency** (no heat in winter, no AC in summer — PANIC), **planned replacement** (system is old, shopping for quotes), or **maintenance** (tune-up, filter change). Emergency visitors are the most valuable and most impatient. They will call the first company that looks trustworthy.

---

## Global Conversion Elements (Persistent on Every Scroll Position)

### Sticky Header
- **Logo** (left)
- **Phone number** (large, bold, click-to-call) — e.g., `(555) 123-4567`
- **"Schedule Service" CTA button** (right, contrasting color) → scrolls to booking form or opens calendar
- **Emergency badge:** `24/7 Emergency Service` (red or orange accent, always visible)
- Hamburger menu (mobile) with anchor links

### Floating Mobile CTA Bar (Mobile Only)
- Fixed bottom of screen
- Three buttons: **Call Now** | **Text Us** | **Book Online**
- Never dismissed, always accessible

### Floating Chat/Text Widget (Bottom Right, Desktop)
- "No heat? No AC? Text us for fast help"
- Opens SMS or webchat

## Section 1: Hero (Above the Fold)

**Purpose:** Immediately communicate two things — "we fix the emergency" AND "we're trustworthy for the big install." Conversion must be possible without scrolling.

### Layout
- Full-width background: Photo of uniformed tech at a home, shaking hands with homeowner or working on a unit. Professional, clean, approachable. NOT a stock photo.
- Dark overlay for text readability

### Content
- **Headline (H1) — Rotate seasonally:**
  - Summer: `AC Not Working? [City]'s Fastest HVAC Response`
  - Winter: `No Heat? [City]'s Trusted Heating Experts — Same-Day Service`
  - Year-round default: `[City]'s Most Trusted Heating & Air Conditioning Company`

- **Subheadline (H2):**
  `Same-Day Service • Licensed & Insured • [X]+ 5-Star Reviews • Financing Available`

- **Trust badges row:**
  - Google rating (stars + count)
  - "Licensed & Insured"
  - Carrier/Trane/Lennox dealer badge (if applicable)
  - BBB Accredited
  - "24/7 Emergency Service"
  - "Financing Available"

- **Primary CTA:**
  `Schedule Service Now →`
  Large, high-contrast button

- **Secondary CTA:**
  `Or call 24/7: (555) 123-4567`

### Mobile
- Headline + CTA visible without scrolling
- Phone number tap-to-call
- Hero image compressed (<200KB)

## Section 2: Emergency Banner

**Purpose:** Catch the person whose furnace just died at 11pm or whose AC quit on a 100-degree day. They need to know you answer NOW.

### Layout
- Full-width, high-contrast banner (red or deep orange)
- Always visible (not seasonal — HVAC emergencies happen year-round)

### Content
- **Icon:** 🔥 or ❄️ (swap seasonally) or generic alert icon
- **Text:** `Heating or cooling emergency? We dispatch 24/7 — including nights, weekends, and holidays.`
- **CTA:** `Call Now: (555) 123-4567`

## Section 3: Services Overview

**Purpose:** Let the visitor self-select their need. Different services, different urgency levels, different conversion paths.

### Layout
- 4 cards in a grid (2x2 on mobile)
- Each card clickable

### Cards

1. **AC Repair & Installation**
   - Icon: snowflake/AC unit
   - Description: "Fast AC repair and energy-efficient system replacement"
   - CTA: "Fix My AC →"

2. **Heating Repair & Installation**
   - Icon: flame/furnace
   - Description: "Furnace, heat pump, and boiler repair and replacement"
   - CTA: "Fix My Heating →"

3. **Maintenance Plans**
   - Icon: calendar with checkmark
   - Description: "Annual tune-ups that prevent breakdowns and lower your bills"
   - CTA: "See Maintenance Plans →"
   - **Highlight badge:** "Most Popular" or "Save $XX/Year"

4. **Indoor Air Quality**
   - Icon: wind/air flow
   - Description: "Air purifiers, humidifiers, duct cleaning, and filtration"
   - CTA: "Improve My Air →"

## Section 4: Current Offer / Seasonal Promotion

**Purpose:** Create urgency and give the visitor a financial reason to act now. HVAC companies live on seasonal promotions — this section makes their current deal prominent.

### Layout
- Full-width section with accent background
- Coupon/offer card design (dashed border, feels like a deal)

### Content (Swap seasonally, examples below)
- **Summer:** `$59 AC Tune-Up Special — Book Before [Date]`
- **Winter:** `$79 Furnace Safety Check — Limited Appointments`
- **Year-round:** `$500 Off Any New System Installation` or `0% Financing for 60 Months`
- **Fine print:** Expiration date, any conditions
- **CTA:** `Claim This Offer →` (links to form/calendar)

### Why This Section Matters
- HVAC is highly seasonal and deal-driven
- Homeowners shop multiple companies — a visible deal can tip the decision
- Creates urgency when paired with limited dates/slots

## Section 6: Social Proof — Reviews

**Purpose:** HVAC is a trust purchase — you're letting a stranger into your home. Reviews crush this objection.

### Layout
- Headline: `Why [City] Homeowners Trust Us`
- 3 review cards (carousel on mobile)
- Each: **stars**, **quote** (2-3 sentences), **name + city**, **"Google Review"** badge
- `Read All [X] Reviews →` (links to Google Business Profile)

### Review Selection Criteria
- Prioritize reviews mentioning: speed of response, fairness of pricing, clean/respectful technicians, problem explained clearly
- Especially valuable: reviews from emergency situations ("our furnace died on Christmas Eve and they came within 2 hours")

## Section 7: Conversion Block #1 — Booking Form + Calendar

**Purpose:** Primary mid-page conversion. Visitor is warmed up — capture them.

### Layout
- Full-width contrasting background
- Two columns desktop: copy left, form/calendar right
- Single column mobile: copy above, form below

### Left Column — Copy
- **Headline:** `Schedule Your Service Call`
- **Subtext:** `Book online in 60 seconds. Most appointments available within 24 hours.`
- **What to expect:**
  - ✓ Certified technician arrives in a marked vehicle, on time
  - ✓ Full diagnosis before any work begins
  - ✓ Upfront pricing — you approve before we start
  - ✓ Your home left cleaner than we found it

### Right Column — Form OR Calendar Toggle

**Option A: Form**
- Fields:
  1. Full Name (required)
  2. Phone Number (required)
  3. Email (required)
  4. Address or Zip Code (required)
  5. What do you need? (dropdown: AC Repair / Heating Repair / New System Estimate / Maintenance / Other)
  6. Is this an emergency? (toggle yes/no — if yes, show: "For fastest service, call us directly: (555) 123-4567")
  7. Preferred contact method (radio: Call / Text / Email)

- **Submit:** `Book My Appointment →`
- **Below:** `Or call us: (555) 123-4567`
- Privacy line

**Option B: Calendar Widget**
- Calendly/Cal.com/GHL embed
- Visitor picks date + time slot
- Auto-confirmation via text + email

**Recommended:** Show BOTH — form as primary, "Or pick a specific time →" link below that opens the calendar

## Section 8: Maintenance Plan Spotlight

**Purpose:** This is a KEY revenue driver unique to HVAC. Maintenance plans = recurring revenue for the client and a reason for the visitor to commit today. Your automation system will manage these renewals.

### Layout
- Section headline: `Protect Your System Year-Round`
- 2 plan cards side by side (Good / Better) or single featured plan
- Comparison table if multiple tiers

### Example Plan Structure

**Comfort Club Membership — $XX/month or $XX/year**

| Included | Details |
|---|---|
| 2 tune-ups per year | Spring AC + Fall Heating |
| Priority scheduling | Front of the line, always |
| 15% off all repairs | Parts and labor |
| No overtime charges | Evenings and weekends at regular price |
| Filter reminders | We remind you AND deliver |

- **CTA:** `Join the Comfort Club →`
- **Urgency line:** `[X] spots remaining this month` (if applicable)

### Why This Section Exists
- Most HVAC sites bury maintenance plans in a sub-page
- Making it prominent converts more members
- Members = recurring revenue + built-in upsell path when their system eventually needs replacement
- Your automation system handles the entire lifecycle: join → schedule → remind → renew

## Section 9: Brands We Service / Install

**Purpose:** Homeowners search for brand-specific repair ("Carrier AC repair [city]"). This section captures that intent AND builds credibility.

### Layout
- Logo strip: Carrier, Trane, Lennox, Rheem, Goodman, Daikin, York, etc.
- Simple headline: `We Service & Install All Major Brands`
- If they're a certified dealer for specific brands, highlight that: "Authorized Carrier Dealer"

---

## Section 10: How It Works

**Purpose:** Demystify the process. Reduce anxiety about cost surprises and pushy upsells.

### Steps
1. **Schedule** — "Book online or call. We confirm within 30 minutes."
2. **Diagnose** — "Our technician inspects your system and explains the issue in plain English."
3. **Approve** — "You see the price upfront. No work starts until you say yes."
4. **Fix or Install** — "We complete the work, test everything, and clean up after ourselves."

---

## Section 11: Financing Information

**Purpose:** A new HVAC system costs $9K–$16K+. Many homeowners can't pay cash. Financing removes the biggest objection to closing a replacement deal.

### Layout
- Clean section with financing partner logo(s)
- Key selling points

### Content
- **Headline:** `Affordable Comfort — Financing Options Available`
- Bullet points:
  - 0% APR options for qualified buyers
  - Monthly payments as low as $XX/month
  - Easy online application — approval in minutes
  - No prepayment penalties
- **CTA:** `Check My Options →` or `Apply in 60 Seconds →`
- Link to financing application (Wells Fargo, Synchrony, GreenSky, etc.)
