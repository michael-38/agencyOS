What is the difference between Botox and Dysport?
Botox and Dysport are both FDA-approved botulinum toxin type A neuromodulators that relax the muscles responsible for dynamic wrinkles. Dysport diffuses slightly more, which can be ideal for larger areas like the forehead and shows results in 2–3 days. Botox stays more localized, ideal for precise placement, and shows results in 3–5 days. Both last roughly 3–4 months. We choose between them based on the area treated and your facial anatomy.
Will Botox or filler make me look "done" or fake?
No. Our entire aesthetic philosophy is restraint. We favor preventive, light-handed work that's invisible as a procedure and only visible as "you, rested." If a treatment risks making you look altered rather than refreshed, we'll advise against it — and often we do.
What does a med spa consultation cost at Revitalize?
Consultations at Revitalize Clinic are complimentary and last about 20 minutes. You meet the physician, ask questions, and leave without any financial commitment before deciding if we're the right clinic for you. Available in-clinic at 429 Fillmore St, or by HIPAA-compliant video call.
How quickly will I see results from injectables, laser, or RF microneedling?
It depends on the treatment. Neuromodulators (Botox, Dysport, Xeomin) begin showing at day 3 and peak around day 14. Dermal fillers are visible immediately with final settling at 2 weeks. Laser and RF microneedling results unfold over 6–12 weeks as collagen remodels, with full benefit at 3 months. We document progress with standardized clinical photography.
Are your practitioners board-certified physicians?
Yes. Every injection, laser, and RF treatment at Revitalize is performed or directly supervised by a board-certified physician (MD, FAAD) or a doctorally-trained nurse practitioner (DNP) with advanced procedural training. Medical Director Dr. Maya Smith is Stanford-trained and FAAD-certified. No handoffs to junior staff.
What treatment is best for acne scars and large pores?
RF microneedling is the most effective non-surgical treatment for acne scars and enlarged pores, combining controlled needle injury with radiofrequency energy to remodel collagen at depth. A typical course is three sessions spaced six weeks apart. For deeper ice-pick scars we may pair RF microneedling with fractional CO₂ laser; for milder texture, glycolic or salicylic peels can complement the protocol.
What's the best treatment for fine lines on the forehead?
For dynamic forehead lines (visible when you raise your brows), neuromodulator injections such as Botox, Dysport, or Daxxify are the gold standard. For static lines visible at rest, we may layer in light hyaluronic-acid filler or a non-ablative laser. Most patients see meaningful softening at day 14 and we recommend a follow-up at 2 weeks to refine.
What if I don't know what treatment I need?
Most of our new patients don't — and that's exactly when to book. Our consults begin with what bothers you, not what we sell. We'll frequently recommend skincare or watchful waiting before any procedure.
Do you offer financing for med spa treatments?
Yes — we accept Cherry and CareCredit for qualified patients, in addition to all major credit cards and HSA/FSA payment for medically eligible procedures. We also publish transparent pricing on every treatment page — no surprise quotes, no pressure in the consult room.
Is there downtime after laser resurfacing or RF microneedling?
Most patients return to normal activity within 24–48 hours. Mild redness and pinpoint scabbing can last 3–5 days for fractional laser and 24 hours for RF microneedling. HydraFacial, neuromodulators, and most injectables have no downtime.
Are aesthetic treatments safe during pregnancy or breastfeeding?
Most aesthetic procedures are deferred during pregnancy and lactation. HydraFacial and certain superficial peels (lactic, mandelic) may be appropriate; injectables, retinoids, and laser are not. We review your medical status at intake and recommend accordingly.
