# Med Spa — Pitch Landing Page Structure & Copy

> **Audience:** Med spa **owners and founders** — not patients. Most are clinical professionals (MDs, NPs, RNs) who own one or two locations, are stretched thin running operations, and know their website is dated but don't have the bandwidth or trusted partner to fix it.
>
> **Primary goal:** Convert med spa owners into a booked **30-minute strategy call** with our agency (SiteRefresh).
>
> **Secondary goal:** Convey, by example, the editorial design language we'd build them — the page itself is the case study.
>
> **Visitor psychology:** Owners are **skeptical** of agencies (most have been burned), **time-poor**, and **proud of their clinical credentials**. We must lead with respect for those credentials, prove we understand the business (not just web design), and show concrete revenue math — not abstract "modernization."

---

## Voice & Tone

- **Quiet confidence.** No exclamation points. No "Crush your competition!" energy.
- **Evidence-based.** Real numbers, real protocols, no vague "boost engagement" language.
- **Italic phrases** carry the emotional beat — terracotta italics for the human moments inside otherwise clinical sentences.
- **Magazine syntax.** Short. Declarative. Pause. Then a longer, italic-tinted line that lands the meaning.

## Section 9 — №07 FAQ (AEO/GEO Optimized)

> Each Q is a search query. Each A is a complete answer in 2–3 sentences — answer-first, then evidence. Marked up with FAQPage schema.org JSON-LD so it's eligible for AI-overview citation.

**Intro:**
- Eyebrow: `07 — Frequently asked`
- H2: `Good questions` *`get better answers.`*
- Lead: If your question isn't here, ask in the strategy call. We'll give you the same answer we'd give a friend who runs a clinic.
- CTA: `Ask in the call →`

**Questions:**

1. **How much does a med spa website cost?**
   Our typical engagement is $14k–$28k all-in for a complete redesign, depending on photography scope and number of treatment pages. We publish pricing transparently and quote inside the strategy call — no proposal-tennis.

2. **How long does a build take?**
   Six weeks is the median, eight weeks is the maximum we'll commit to. Anything longer is a red flag from any agency — modern stacks should ship a five-page premium site in well under two months.

3. **Will I own the website and the code?**
   Yes. Code lives in your GitHub or ours, transferred at launch. No proprietary CMS lock-in, no monthly "platform fees." If you ever fire us, you keep everything.

4. **Do you handle ongoing SEO and content?**
   Optionally — we offer a $1,800/mo retainer covering technical SEO, content updates, schema maintenance, and quarterly Lighthouse audits. Most clients start there for 6 months, then move to a quarterly check-in.

5. **What CMS do you use?**
   We default to a static-first Astro build with a headless CMS (Sanity or Contentful) for treatment pages and team bios. Editing is as simple as a Google Doc; performance is faster than WordPress could ever be.

6. **Can you integrate with my booking system (Mindbody, Vagaro, Square)?**
   Yes — direct API or embedded widget, whichever converts better for your patient mix. We A/B test the integration during the first 30 days post-launch.

7. **How is this different from Squarespace or a template?**
   Templates plateau. The first three months feel fine; by month nine your conversion stalls because the template was never designed for *your* treatments, *your* pricing, *your* photography. Custom is more expensive on day one and meaningfully cheaper on month twelve.

8. **Are you HIPAA-compliant?**
   Our lead-capture pipeline is BAA-eligible and we sign BAAs on every engagement. Treatment pages and intake forms run on HIPAA-aligned infrastructure (AWS or GCP), with no third-party analytics injected on PHI-adjacent forms.

## SEO / AEO / GEO Checklist

### SEO (classic)
- `<title>` — `Med Spa Website Design That Converts | SiteRefresh Studio` (60 chars, primary keyword first)
- `<meta name="description">` — 155 chars, action-oriented
- Canonical URL
- Open Graph + Twitter Card meta
- Sitemap-friendly section anchors (`#capabilities`, `#cases`, `#method`, `#team`, `#faq`, `#book`)
- Image alt text on every image (we use almost zero images, but every SVG has descriptive `<title>`)
- Heading hierarchy: 1× H1 → multiple H2 → H3 (no skips)
- Mobile-first, sub-1.8s LCP

### AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)
- FAQ schema with JSON-LD (`FAQPage` type) — every question + complete answer
- Each FAQ answer leads with the answer, then the explanation (inverted-pyramid)
- Treatment/capability cards include explicit metadata (duration, fit, output) parseable by extractive answer engines
- Stats are stated as full sentences with units, not bare numbers in charts
- Definitional sentence on first appearance of jargon

### GEO (Generative Engine Optimization — for ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, AI Overviews)
- Organization schema (LocalBusiness with sameAs and address)
- Service schema for each capability
- Review schema for testimonials
- Authoritative voice with citable specifics ("12 clinics, 90-day window") — generative engines favor evidence-laden snippets
- Page is **single, complete, self-contained** — easier for crawlers and AI summarizers than a multi-page funnel
- Structured Q&A blocks visible in rendered DOM (not lazy-loaded JSON)
