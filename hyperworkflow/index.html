How the sprint works

Ten working days. One workflow shipped.

We can't automate work from process diagrams alone — we have to see how the work really gets done. So the sprint starts inside your team, not in a slide deck.

01 · Days 1–2

Shadow the expert

We sit with the person doing the work and observe every step of the real thing — documenting workflows, asking questions, building intuition for how the job actually gets done.

02 · Day 3

Prioritize opportunities

We rank everything we saw by scale, repetition, business impact, and data availability.

03 · Days 4–5

Build together

A working agent takes shape alongside your expert while they do the real job — built with them, not for them.

04 · Days 6–9

Validate and test

We test whether the agent generalizes — and whether it genuinely makes the job better, not just faster.

05 · Day 10

Ship

The agent goes live in your stack. Your team knows exactly how it works, because they built it with us.

What you walk away with

  • A working agent in production
  • A ranked automation map
  • Validation evidence, not a demo

How we think

Three principles behind every sprint.

What we've learned shipping agents inside real teams.

Automate workflows, not tasks.

The biggest wins come from rethinking an entire workflow — eliminating handoffs, removing unnecessary approvals, replacing legacy tooling — not from speeding up one step. The workflow becomes the unit of automation.

Opportunities are invisible from the outside.

The best use cases hide in plain sight, and they only surface by sitting next to the people doing the work. That's why every sprint starts with shadowing, not scoping.

Institutionalize the pattern, then hand it off.

Two weeks captures the low-hanging fruit. The bigger payoff comes after — when your team, now fluent in agentic thinking, redesigns its own work and reinvests the reclaimed time into work that wasn't possible before.

What is the Agentic Implementation program?

A two-week sprint where we pair with your domain experts — the people who actually do the work. We shadow the real job, rank the automation opportunities, build a working agent alongside your team, validate it on real work, and ship on day 10.

Why two weeks?

Long enough to observe real work, build, and validate — short enough to force decisions. The sprint is designed to get your first real win into production fast, not to produce a report.

How much of our expert's time does it take?

Days 1–2 are the heaviest — we shadow them doing their normal work, so it costs attention, not extra hours. After that, expect a couple of focused hours a day for building and validating together.

What do we walk away with?

A working agent in production, a ranked map of your automation opportunities, validation evidence from real work, and a team that knows how to do this again.

What happens after the sprint?

Whatever you want: extend with follow-on sprints, keep us on as an ongoing partner, or take it fully in-house. The handoff is designed in from day one.

Do we own what you build?

Yes. The code, the prompts, the integrations, the data, and the accounts. No lock-in.

What about security and data access?

Least-privilege access, working in your environment wherever possible, and human review on what the agent does. We'll walk through the specifics on the call.

What if the agent doesn't validate?

Then we don't ship it. Days 6–9 exist to find that out before it touches real work — and you keep the opportunity map, the documentation, and everything we learned.

What kinds of work fit best?

Repetitive knowledge work in any function — intake, triage, drafting, reconciliation, reporting, scheduling. If someone can show us the work, we can usually shadow it.

How much does it cost?

We scope it on the call. The first call is free, and you'll leave with a clear recommendation either way.
