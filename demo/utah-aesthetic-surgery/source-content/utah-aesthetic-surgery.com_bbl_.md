### OVERVIEW

**Buttock fat transfer—commonly referred to as a _Brazilian Butt Lift (BBL)_—is an advanced body contouring procedure that:**

- Enhances the **shape, volume, and projection** of the buttocks
- Uses your **own natural fat** (no implants)
- Simultaneously **sculpts the waist, abdomen, and flanks**

👉 The result is a more defined **hourglass silhouette** with natural look and feel

### HOW THE PROCEDURE WORKS

**Step 1: Fat Harvesting (Liposuction)**

- Fat is gently removed from areas such as:
  - Abdomen
  - Flanks (love handles)
  - Lower back

**Step 2: Fat Processing**

- Fat is purified and prepared for transfer

**Step 3: Fat Transfer**

- Fat is carefully injected into the buttocks
- Focus on:
  - Shape
  - Projection
  - Symmetry

👉 Only **safe, controlled placement techniques** are used

You may be a good candidate if you:

**Not ideal if:**

Unable to follow recovery instructions

## SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS (VERY IMPORTANT)

Buttock fat transfer is a technically advanced procedure that requires strict adherence to safety protocols.

At our practice:

- Fat is placed **only in the subcutaneous (safe) layer**
- **No intramuscular injection** is performed
- Careful control of cannula placement at all times
- Emphasis on **patient positioning and technique**

👉 These principles align with **current safety recommendations in plastic surgery**

## BBL Risks And Complications

**All surgery carries risk. Specific risks include:**

- Fat resorption (loss of some volume)
- Asymmetry
- Infection
- Seroma (fluid collection)
- Contour irregularities
- Fat embolism (rare but serious)

👉 Strict adherence to **modern safety technique significantly reduces risk**

## RESULTS

**Patients can expect:**

- Fuller, more lifted buttocks
- Slimmer waistline
- Improved body proportions
- Natural look and feel

Final results develop over **3–6 months**

## LONG-TERM MAINTENANCE

- Maintain stable weight
- Healthy lifestyle supports long-term results
- Significant weight changes can affect outcome

## COMMON QUESTIONS

Is this better than implants?

For many patients, yes—fat transfer looks and feels more natural.

Will all the fat survive?

No—some is naturally reabsorbed. We account for this during surgery.

Will I have scars?

Only very small liposuction incisions.

How long do results last?

Long-lasting, especially with stable weight.
