### OVERVIEW

Rhinoplasty is a surgical procedure designed to improve the **shape, proportion, and balance of the nose** while maintaining harmony with the rest of the face.

It may address:

- Dorsal hump (bridge prominence)
- Nasal asymmetry
- Bulbous or drooping tip
- Wide or flared nostrils
- Functional concerns (breathing issues, in select cases)

👉 The goal is a **natural, balanced result—not an obvious or overdone change**

### The Utah Rhinoplasty Surgery Approach

At **Utah Aesthetic Surgery**, rhinoplasty is approached with:

- **Facial harmony as the priority**—not isolated nasal change
- Respect for **individual anatomy and ethnicity**
- Conservative refinement rather than aggressive alteration
- A focus on **long-term structural integrity**

Dr. Rodrigues performs rhinoplasty with an emphasis on **precision, proportion, and natural outcomes**, achieving high patient satisfaction among Utah patients.

## Nasal Anatomy & Aesthetic Principles

![Nasal Anatomy & Aesthetic Principles](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/efefef.png)

A balanced nose depends on:

- **Bone (upper third)**
- **Cartilage (middle and tip)**
- **Skin thickness and quality**

Key aesthetic relationships include:

- Smooth nasal bridge
- Defined but natural tip
- Appropriate projection and rotation
- Harmony with chin, lips, and overall facial structure

👉 Rhinoplasty is as much **art as it is surgery**

![overall facial structure](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/efefefef.png)

## TYPES OF RHINOPLASTY

**Cosmetic Rhinoplasty**

- Improves shape and appearance

**Functional Rhinoplasty**

- Addresses breathing issues (septum, valves)

**Revision Rhinoplasty**

- Corrects or refines previous surgery

## SURGICAL TECHNIQUES

![Open Rhinoplasty](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/sffsff.jpg)

**Open Rhinoplasty**

- Small incision at the columella (between nostrils)
- Allows precise visualization and control

**Closed Rhinoplasty**

- All incisions inside the nose
- No external scar

**Common Techniques Include:**

- Dorsal hump reduction
- Tip refinement (reshaping cartilage)
- Structural grafting for support
- Osteotomies (controlled bone repositioning)

👉 Modern rhinoplasty focuses on **structure and support—not just reduction**

### CANDIDACY

You may be a good candidate if you:

- Are bothered by nasal shape or proportion
- Have realistic expectations
- Are in good general health

Have completed facial growth (typically late teens+)

## Rhinoplasty Recovery

![Rhinoplasty Recovery](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/jhhhjhjh.jpg)

**First Week**

- Nasal splint in place
- Swelling and bruising (especially around eyes)

**1–2 Weeks**

- Splint removed
- Return to light activities

**2–4 Weeks**

- Most visible swelling improves

**3–12 Months**

- Continued refinement, especially of the tip

👉 Final results take **time—patience is essential**

## Rhinoplasty Risks and Safety

All surgery carries risk. Specific considerations include:

- Swelling and prolonged healing
- Asymmetry
- Breathing changes
- Need for revision (in a small percentage of patients)
- Scarring (minimal and usually well hidden)

👉 Careful planning and technique help minimize these risks

### COMMON QUESTIONS

Will my nose look “done”?

No—our goal is refinement that blends naturally with your face.

Is rhinoplasty painful?

Discomfort is usually mild and well-controlled.

How long until I see results?

Initial improvement is seen early, but full refinement can take up to a year.

Can breathing be improved?

Yes—functional concerns can often be addressed at the same time.
