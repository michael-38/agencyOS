### OVERVIEW

**Face and neck rejuvenation—commonly referred to as a _facelift_—is designed to restore a more youthful, refreshed appearance by addressing:**

- Sagging skin of the cheeks and jawline
- Jowling along the lower face
- Neck laxity and banding
- Loss of facial contour and definition

👉 The goal is **natural rejuvenation—not a “tight” or overdone appearance**

### WHAT AGING CHANGES IN THE FACE

![Facelift Aging Process](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/fefeff.jpg)

![](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/efeeefefe.png)

Facial aging is not just skin—it involves deeper changes:

- **Bone resorption** → loss of structural support
- **Fat loss and descent** → hollowing and sagging
- **Skin laxity** → wrinkles and folds
- **Muscle changes (platysma)** → neck banding

👉 Effective rejuvenation must address **all layers—not just skin tightening**

![Facial aging](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/zfzfzf.png)

## THE PROCEDURE: LAYERED REJUVENATION

![Facelift Sutures](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/assassss.jpg)

![Facelift ligament](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/dvvvvvvv.png)

![Facelift Relevant Anatomy](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/sdsdsdsd.png)

Modern facelift techniques focus on **structural repositioning**, not just skin pulling.

**Key Components:**

- **SMAS / Deep Tissue Lift**
  - Repositions underlying facial structures
  - Provides durable, natural results
- **Skin Redraping**
  - Excess skin is conservatively adjusted
  - Avoids “wind-swept” appearance
- **Neck Rejuvenation**
  - Tightening of platysma muscle
  - Improvement of jawline and neck contour

## INCISION PLACEMENT (DISCREET DESIGN)

![Skin redundancy](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/zssssssss.png)

**Incisions are carefully placed:**

- Along natural creases around the ear
- Within the hairline when appropriate
- Occasionally under the chin (for neck work)

👉 Designed to be **well-hidden and minimally visible**

## **ADJUNCTIVE PROCEDURES (COMMONLY COMBINED)**

Facial rejuvenation is often enhanced with:

- **Eyelid surgery (blepharoplasty)**
- **Fat grafting (volume restoration)**
- **Laser or skin resurfacing**
- **Brow repositioning (when appropriate)**

👉 A comprehensive approach yields the most **natural and balanced outcome**

## **CANDIDACY**

You may be a good candidate if you:

- Have visible facial or neck laxity
- Desire a long-lasting rejuvenation
- Are in good overall health
- Have realistic expectations

**Typical age range:** 40s–70s (varies widely)

## Facelift Recovery

![Facelift Recovery](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/afefeafafea.jpg)

**First 1–2 Weeks**

- Swelling and bruising
- Head elevation recommended
- Return to light activities

**2–4 Weeks**

- Social recovery (most patients comfortable in public)

**4–6 Weeks**

- Resume full activities

**3–6 Months**

- Final refinement of results

## Facelift Risks and Safety

![Facelift Recovery](https://utah-aesthetic-surgery.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/fefefefefefefsfsf.png)

While generally safe, risks include:

- Bruising and swelling
- Hematoma (blood collection)
- Temporary or rare nerve weakness
- Scarring
- Skin healing issues

👉 Careful technique and postoperative monitoring help minimize risk

### COMMON QUESTIONS

Will I look “pulled” or unnatural?

No—modern techniques focus on repositioning, not over-tightening.

How visible are scars?

Typically very well hidden and fade significantly over time.

Is this better than fillers?

For moderate to advanced aging, surgery provides **more definitive and lasting results**.

When can I return to work?

Most patients return within 2–3 weeks depending on comfort level.
