Homepage missing <title> tag SEO (on-page)
critical
Search engines and social previews show a blank title.
Add a 50–60 char title including clinic name + primary city/service.
Zero pricing or cost signals anywhere on the site LLM copy / AEO
critical
Not a single page contains a price, price range, or 'starting at' figure for any procedure (BBL, breast augmentation, tummy tuck, rhinoplasty, blepharoplasty, facelift). LLMs answering 'How much does a BBL cost in South Jordan, UT?' cannot cite this site at all.
Add a 'Pricing' section to each procedure page and to the homepage. Minimum viable example: 'Brazilian Butt Lift (BBL) at Utah Aesthetic Surgery starts at $8,500–$14,000 depending on the volume of liposuction required. Breast augmentation starts at $6,500–$10,000 including implants, anesthesia, and facility fees. Tummy tuck (abdominoplasty) starts at $7,500. Exact quotes are provided at your complimentary consultation.' Even broad ranges dramatically improve AEO citability.
No pricing or cost information for any procedure Copy & conversion
critical
Not one procedure page or the homepage discloses a price, range, or 'starting at' figure. High-intent buyers comparison-shopping surgeons in the Salt Lake area will immediately leave for competitors who show pricing. The 'Contact us for pricing' model is an invisible wall at the top of the funnel.
Add a 'What Does [Procedure] Cost?' section to each page. Paste-ready example for breast augmentation: 'Breast augmentation at Utah Aesthetic Surgery starts at $6,500–$10,000, which includes surgeon fees, anesthesia, implants, and facility costs. The final quote depends on implant type (silicone vs. saline), placement technique, and surgical complexity. We provide an itemized quote at your free consultation.' Mirror this pattern for every other procedure.
No dedicated 'Book a Consultation' CTA — only generic contact form links Copy & conversion
critical
Every 'Make An Appointment' and 'Get Started' link resolves to /contact-us/, which shows a blank contact form with no context, confirmation language, or reassurance copy. There is no button or page specifically labeled 'Book a Free Consultation' or 'Schedule Your Consult.' Generic contact links do not convert as well as consultation-specific CTAs.
Rename the CTA button across all pages to 'Book Your Free Consultation' and add a sentence above the contact form on /contact-us/ reading: 'Schedule your complimentary consultation with Dr. Robert Rodrigues. During your visit, you'll receive a personalized surgical plan, full pricing quote, and answers to all your questions — with no obligation to book.' Also add an inline CTA at the bottom of each procedure page: 'Ready to explore [procedure name]? Call (801) 810-0761 or click below to book your free consultation with Dr. Rodrigues in South Jordan, UT.'

Dr. Rodrigues's credentials are vague — board certification bodies never named LLM copy / AEO
high
The site repeatedly says 'double board-certified' and 'board-certified plastic surgeon' but never names the certifying boards (e.g., American Board of Plastic Surgery, American Board of Surgery). LLMs require specific, verifiable named entities to cite authoritatively.
On the Meet the Doctor page and homepage bio, add: 'Dr. Robert Rodrigues is double board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery (ABPS) and the American Board of Surgery (ABS). He completed his plastic surgery residency at [Institution Name] and has practiced aesthetic surgery in South Jordan, Utah for [X] years.' Also add his medical school and any fellowship training.
City and neighborhood almost never appear in body copy — geo-cues confined to footer address LLM copy / AEO
high
Across all procedure pages (BBL, blepharoplasty, breast augmentation, facelift, etc.), 'South Jordan' and 'Utah' appear only in the footer address block and occasionally in a tagline. Body paragraphs never situate the clinic geographically. LLMs answering 'best plastic surgeon near South Jordan' cannot confidently cite pages that lack in-body geo-signals.
Insert a geo-anchored sentence into the opening paragraph of every procedure page. Example for the BBL page: 'Dr. Robert Rodrigues performs Brazilian Butt Lift surgery at Utah Aesthetic Surgery, located at 10382 S. Jordan Gateway, Suite 100, in South Jordan, Utah—conveniently serving patients throughout Salt Lake County, Draper, Riverton, and the greater Salt Lake City area.'
No sitewide FAQ hub — per-page 'Common Questions' blocks are minimal (3–4 Q&As) and lack specificity LLM copy / AEO
high
Each procedure page has a 3–5 item 'Common Questions' section, but answers are very brief and generic (e.g., 'Long-lasting, especially with stable weight'). There is no dedicated FAQ page, and questions do not match real user search queries like 'How long does BBL recovery take?' or 'Does breast augmentation feel natural?'
Create a standalone /faq/ page and expand each procedure's FAQ to at least 8 Q&As with substantive answers. Example for BBL: 'Q: How long is BBL recovery in South Jordan, UT? A: Most Utah Aesthetic Surgery BBL patients avoid direct sitting for 2 weeks, use a BBL pillow for weeks 2–6, and return to desk work within 10–14 days. Full results stabilize at 3–6 months, with 60–80% of transferred fat surviving long-term.'
Credentials listed vaguely — certifying boards, training institutions, and years of experience omitted Copy & conversion
high
Dr. Rodrigues is described as 'double board-certified' across multiple pages, but the certifying board names, his medical school, residency program, fellowship (if any), and number of years in practice are never stated. One testimonial mentions she's been a patient since 2008, implying 15+ years of experience — but this is not stated in the doctor's bio. Buyers cross-shopping surgeons look for specific credentials.
Add a credential block to the Meet the Doctor page and embed a shorter version on the homepage. Example: 'Dr. Robert Rodrigues, MD — Double Board-Certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery and the American Board of Surgery. [Medical School], [Residency Institution]. Practicing aesthetic and reconstructive plastic surgery in South Jordan, Utah since [year]. Over [X] procedures performed.' If a fellowship exists, name the institution and specialty.
No financing or payment plan information anywhere on the site Copy & conversion
high
Plastic surgery procedures costing $6,500–$15,000+ require financing for many patients. The site makes no mention of CareCredit, Cherry, Alphaeon, in-house payment plans, or any financing option. This is a significant conversion blocker for cost-sensitive prospects.
Add a 'Financing' section to the homepage and each procedure page. Example: 'Utah Aesthetic Surgery works with CareCredit and Cherry to offer flexible monthly payment plans. Many patients finance their procedure for as little as $X/month with approved credit. Ask about financing options during your consultation or call (801) 810-0761.' Apply to enroll with CareCredit at carecredit.com/providers if not already enrolled.
No parking, transit, or neighborhood context on the location/contact page Copy & conversion
high
The address (10382 S. Jordan Gateway, Suite 100, South Jordan, UT 84095) appears in the footer and on the contact page, but there is no information about parking availability, proximity to landmarks, freeway access (I-15 exit), or nearby cities served. This creates friction for first-time visitors.
Add a location block to /contact-us/: 'Utah Aesthetic Surgery is located on S. Jordan Gateway in South Jordan, UT, just off the [nearest I-15 exit] exit — approximately [X] minutes south of downtown Salt Lake City and [X] minutes from Draper and Riverton. Free parking is available in the building lot. Suite 100 is located on the ground floor.' Include a Google Maps embed.
